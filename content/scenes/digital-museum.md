# The Power of Nuclear Energy

What if one fuel pellet could replace a ton of coal?

Nuclear energy is one of the most powerful and reliable sources of electricity on Earth. A single uranium fuel pellet, roughly the size of a gummy bear, contains as much energy as one ton of coal, 17,000 cubic feet of natural gas, or 149 gallons of oil.

**440+ operating reactors** across **32 countries** generate about **10% of global electricity** with near-zero carbon emissions.

## Energy Density

A uranium pellet stores an outsized amount of energy compared with fossil fuels.

**1 pellet = 1 ton of coal = 17,000 cubic feet of natural gas = 149 gallons of oil.**

That density lets nuclear plants run for long stretches with comparatively little fuel and a much smaller physical fuel footprint than fossil systems.

Sources: [Visual Capitalist](https://elements.visualcapitalist.com/the-power-of-a-uranium-pellet/), [IAEA](https://www.iaea.org/newscenter/news/what-is-nuclear-energy-the-science-of-nuclear-power)

## How a Reactor Makes Electricity

Most commercial plants use pressurized water reactors. Fission creates heat, the heat makes steam in a separate loop, the steam spins a turbine, and the generator turns that motion into electricity.

**Fission -> heat. Steam generation. Turbine and generator. Cooling and recycling.**

The water that touches the reactor core stays isolated from the turbine loop, adding another engineered barrier while the cycle repeats continuously.

Sources: [Nuclear Energy Institute](https://www.nei.org/fundamentals/how-a-nuclear-reactor-works), [NRC](https://www.nrc.gov/reactors/power/pwrs), [DOE](https://www.energy.gov/ne/articles/nuclear-101-how-does-nuclear-reactor-work)

## Why Nuclear Beats Fossil Fuels

When measured by deaths per unit of energy, lifecycle emissions, and capacity factor, nuclear consistently ranks among the safest and cleanest major sources of electricity.

**~93% capacity factor** keeps nuclear plants online more reliably than any other major source of electricity generation.

**0.03 deaths per TWh for nuclear** versus **2.8 for natural gas**, **18.4 for oil**, and **24.6 for coal** puts routine fossil-fuel harm in perspective.

Lifecycle CO2 emissions are comparable to wind and solar while nuclear still delivers firm 24/7 output.

Sources: [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/safest-sources-of-energy), [IAEA Clean Energy](https://www.iaea.org/sites/default/files/2025-09/cleanenergy_0.pdf), [EIA](https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/nuclear/)

## Addressing Nuclear Safety and Waste

Public discussion often centers on Chernobyl and Fukushima, but peer-reviewed summaries show the long-term death toll from nuclear accidents remains far below the ongoing health burden from fossil-fuel air pollution.

**Chernobyl is commonly cited at about 4,000 projected long-term deaths**, while **Fukushima caused one confirmed radiation fatality**.

All U.S. spent fuel from more than 60 years of commercial reactor operation would fit on a single football field, stacked less than 10 yards high.

Spent fuel is stored in steel-lined pools and dry casks under continuous NRC oversight.

Sources: [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/what-was-the-death-toll-from-chernobyl-and-fukushima), [NRC Spent Fuel Storage](https://www.nrc.gov/waste/spent-fuel-storage/index.html), [Our World in Data Safety Comparison](https://ourworldindata.org/safest-sources-of-energy), [Generation Atomic](https://www.generationatomic.org/why-nuclear/)

## From Uranium to Electricity

Nuclear fuel follows one of the most tightly regulated industrial lifecycles in energy, from mining to reactor operation and long-term storage.

**Mining and milling:** uranium ore becomes yellowcake. **Conversion and enrichment:** uranium hexafluoride is enriched from about 0.7% to 3-5% U-235. **Fuel fabrication:** ceramic pellets are stacked into fuel rods. **Reactor operation:** controlled fission generates heat for electricity. **Storage and disposal:** spent fuel moves from pools to dry casks designed for decades of containment.

Understanding that full lifecycle removes much of the mystery around how nuclear energy becomes carbon-free electricity at industrial scale.

Sources: [EIA Fuel Cycle](https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/nuclear/the-nuclear-fuel-cycle.php), [IAEA Science of Nuclear Power](https://www.iaea.org/newscenter/news/what-is-nuclear-energy-the-science-of-nuclear-power), [NRC Spent Fuel Storage](https://www.nrc.gov/waste/spent-fuel-storage/index.html)

## Powering AI and the Future Grid

AI data centers are one of the fastest-growing sources of electricity demand, and they require round-the-clock, high-density, low-carbon power.

**~30% annual growth** in data center demand could push the sector to **up to 8% of total U.S. electricity by 2030**.

Small modular reactors are designed for incremental, factory-built deployment near demand centers, while nuclear's stable 24/7 output can complement renewables on a more electrified grid.

Sources: [Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/power-and-utilities/nuclear-energy-powering-data-centers.html), [DOE Small Modular Reactors](https://www.energy.gov/ne/small-modular-reactors), [IAEA Smart, Stable, Reliable](https://www.iaea.org/bulletin/smart-stable-reliable)

## The Rise of Nuclear Power

**1942 - Chicago Pile-1.** Enrico Fermi's team achieves the first controlled nuclear chain reaction.

**1954 - Obninsk, USSR.** The first nuclear power plant connected to an electrical grid proves fission can generate usable electricity.

**1957 - Shippingport, Pennsylvania.** The first full-scale commercial nuclear power plant in the United States begins operation.

**1979 - Three Mile Island.** A partial meltdown prompts major safety reforms across the U.S. nuclear industry; no deaths resulted.

**1986 - Chernobyl.** Design flaws and operator error cause the worst nuclear accident in history and reset global safety standards.

**2011 - Fukushima Daiichi.** Tsunami-driven meltdowns trigger worldwide safety reviews; no deaths from radiation exposure were recorded.

**2020s - Nuclear Renaissance.** SMRs, AI demand, and net-zero planning return nuclear to the center of energy strategy.

Nuclear energy is not hypothetical. It is a proven, global-scale, low-carbon power source, and the evidence is here to verify.
