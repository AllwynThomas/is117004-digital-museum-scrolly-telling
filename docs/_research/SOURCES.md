Museum Concept: Nuclear Energy Generation

Explanation: My museum will focus on the benefits of nuclear energy production compared to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. It will also give a general idea of how energy is produced in nuclear reactors since it is not common knowledge. The museum can be helpful for people who are uninformed about nuclear power and can help address common misconceptions about nuclear energy and its safety. It is important to stay informed on this topic because energy needs will be increasing in the future as more clean, reliable power is needed for AI datacenters.

Sources:

- ![uranium vs fossil fuels diagram](../../public/assets/images/uranium_vs_fossil_fuels_diagram.png)https://elements.visualcapitalist.com/the-power-of-a-uranium-pellet
  - This diagram helps visualize the energy densities of different fuel sources.
  - It helps the reader understand how much fossil fuel is needed to produce the same amount of energy as one uranium pellet in a nuclear reactor's fuel assembly.
- ![death and emission comparison for energy sources](../../public/assets/images/safest_cleanest_sources_of_energy_chart.png) https://ourworldindata.org/safest-sources-of-energy
  - This chart depicts the differences in the death rate and greenhouse gas emissions for different energy sources.
  - It helps the reader conceptualize how much safer nuclear energy is compared to fossil fuels. Nuclear energy produces magnitudes less pollution and deaths per terawatt-hour of electricity compared to fossil fuels. This is important to visualize because most people think of major nuclear disasters in history that caused many direct deaths but do not consider the long-term health effects of air pollution by fossil fuels causing many more indirect deaths.
- ![PWR reactor animation](../../public/assets/images/nuclearplant.gif) https://www.nei.org/news/2019/how-reactor-actually-works
  - This animation shows how a pressurized water reactor (PWR) works.
  - It helps the reader visualize the electricity generating process for the most common type of nuclear reactor used today. Most people do not realize it is just a sophisticated way to efficiently heat up water into steam that turns a turbine.
